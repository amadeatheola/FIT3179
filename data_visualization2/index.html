Rice in Southeast Asia

The backbone of Southeast Asia’s agriculture

Consumption

  • ↑ Global rice consumption: nearly 500 million tons
  • 6 ASEAN countries consumed >200 kg per capita annually
    (Vietnam, Thailand, Myanmar, Cambodia, Indonesia, Philippines)
  • Rice = 60–75% of daily calorie intake
  • Pandemic stockpiling & affordability reinforced rice as:
    → Staple grain
    → Cultural foundation

Yield

  • ↑ Global production: rose from ~498M → >515M tons (milled basis)
  • Indonesia & Philippines: steady yields
  • Thailand & Vietnam: drought-related drops
  • Despite climate stress, the region maintained:
    → Stable global supply
    → Strong food security leadership

Export

  • Global rice exports rose, peaking at 55.47M tonnes (2022).
  • India dominated exports, affecting Southeast Asian market share.
  • Prices rose in 2020–2021, benefiting Thai and Vietnamese farmers.
  • Broken rice exports grew — especially to Africa and China.
  • Extreme weather in Laos and Myanmar impacted harvest reliability.
  • Export policies tightened to secure domestic food needs.
  • Rice stayed central to ASEAN economies and rural livelihoods.

Import

  • Philippines became world’s top rice importer during this period.
  • Indonesia and Malaysia relied on imports for food security.
  • Rice demand rose during lockdowns, leading to bulk imports.
  • Supply chain issues delayed rice shipments across ASEAN ports.
  • Imported broken rice used for industrial and animal feed use.
  • Countries diversified import sources beyond Thailand and Vietnam.
  • ASEAN governments built up reserves fearing export bans.
  • Smaller nations like Brunei increased reliance on import partners.
  • Rice remains a strategic import for regional food stability.
